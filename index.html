An Inquiry into Regulation, Agency, and Self-Governance

Thomas J. Hafner · February 2026

Meet Tom Read or Listen to The Foundational Essay

Information exchange shapes every system.

How we regulate perception internally shapes the stability of our personal lives.
How we regulate discourse externally shapes the stability of our institutions.

When perception narrows and feedback weakens, systems destabilize.
When regulation strengthens and exchange becomes reciprocal, stability emerges.

This project develops a working thesis:

Tension → Attention → Regulation → Metacognition → Agency → Reciprocal Exchange → Stability

The regulatory capacity of individuals shapes the stability of institutions.

Self-governance begins with the regulation of perception.

This framework applies across scales — from the nervous system to democratic society.

These dynamics are not new. Humans have long struggled with perception, illusion, and meaning. What has changed is scale. Our information environment now moves faster than our capacity to metabolize it.

The Credo of Flip the Discourse

A short set of commitments for how I will write, listen, and revise.

If you believe I have failed to uphold these commitments, I invite you to let me know.

Email Me
  • I do not claim certainty.
  • I claim responsibility.
  • I will examine claims before I amplify them.
  • I will seek primary sources before conclusions.
  • I will steelman positions before critiquing them.
  • I believe tension arises when information channels close.
  • I believe disciplined dialogue can reopen them.
  • I will update when evidence changes my mind.
  • I will admit error when I am wrong.
  • I will not dehumanize those with whom I disagree.
  • The goal is not victory.
  • The goal is clarity.
