Meet Tom

Who I am, why I’m building this, and the credo that holds it together.

I’m Tom Hafner. I’m interested in how complex systems regulate — and how they fail. Flip The Discourse is an attempt to examine tension, attention, regulation, and reciprocal exchange with both scientific discipline and lived honesty.

For many years, my personal system was far from regulated. That dysregulation affected not only myself, but those around me.

Recovery required attention — attention to the tension I would have rather ignored. It required regulation — a sustained effort in a tense world. It required metacognition — admitting faults and questioning beliefs.

Over time, that awareness expanded my agency, which had previously been constrained. Agency expanded my range of choices. Those choices changed the direction of my life.

I am still learning, and I intend to continue learning through this project. My hope is that this work becomes a thoughtful and enduring resource — not only for myself, but for others navigating similar terrain.

We are living in a moment where public exchange is often rigid and reactive. This project explores whether regulated awareness can scale — from the individual to institutions — toward more reciprocal exchange and greater stability.

When regulation weakens at scale within individuals, the capacity for societal self-governance weakens as well.

The Credo of Flip the Discourse

A short set of commitments for how I will write, listen, and revise.

If you believe I have failed to uphold these commitments, I invite you to let me know.

Email Me
  • I do not claim certainty.
  • I claim responsibility.
  • I will examine claims before I amplify them.
  • I will seek primary sources before conclusions.
  • I will steelman positions before critiquing them.
  • I believe tension arises when information channels close.
  • I believe disciplined dialogue can reopen them.
  • I will update when evidence changes my mind.
  • I will admit error when I am wrong.
  • I will not dehumanize those with whom I disagree.
  • The goal is not victory.
  • The goal is clarity.
